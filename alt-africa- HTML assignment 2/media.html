From Courtrooms to Code: My Spaghetti-Fueled Journey into Backend Engineering

I’m a Law graduate who’s decided to swap courtroom dramas for backend engineering. Why? Because I realized I’d much rather debug code than argue with people (most of the time). Growing up as the firstborn of three, I was basically my parents’ beta test—federal universities weren’t the best idea, but hey, I survived (barely).

During my time at the University of Lagos (UNILAG), I was consistently in the top 5% of my class. I’d love to say it’s because I’m a genius, but let’s be real—it’s probably because I overthink everything. My legal background taught me critical thinking, attention to detail, and how to structure arguments—skills that are surprisingly handy when I’m staring at a screen, trying to figure out why my code won’t compile.

These days, I’m diving into backend engineering, where I get to solve problems, work with databases, and build APIs. It’s like being a digital architect, except I don’t have to wear a hard hat. I’m teaching myself by building small projects, experimenting with Python and SQL, and figuring things out as I go. It’s challenging, but I’m loving every bit of it.

When I’m not wrestling with code, you’ll probably find me eating spaghetti (my ultimate love language) or rewatching Avatar: The Last Airbender for the 47th time. And don’t even get me started on Attack on Titan—that show emotionally wrecked me, but I’d do it all over again. Also, let’s settle this once and for all: Katara is the best character in Avatar. Fight me.

I’m especially interested in using tech to solve real-world problems, like improving access to legal resources through tech-driven solutions. If you’re as passionate about tech (or food) as I am, let’s connect! I’m always open to new ideas, collaborations, or just chatting about why Katara is the GOAT.
